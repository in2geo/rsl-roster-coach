## ‚ñ∂ THE FINDING ‚Äî the engine reads correct content as a checkbox

**The experiment (do not lose this).** The engine recommended an Ice Golem team. Mike ran it at
**Stage 20** ‚Äî the stage he actually clears ‚Äî to separate *team selection* from *stage prediction*:

| Team | Composition | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Mike's** (5 captures) | Xenomorph ¬∑ Ezio ¬∑ Michelangelo ¬∑ Donatello ¬∑ **Duchess Lilitu** | **151-198s** |
| **Engine's** (1 capture) | Ezio ¬∑ Michelangelo ¬∑ Donatello ¬∑ **Sun Wukong** ¬∑ **Alice** | **241s**, Sun Wukong DIED |

**4 of 5 seats identical.** The engine swapped **Xenomorph ‚Üí Sun Wukong** and cost **~60% of clear
time** plus a death. Xenomorph is **top damage in all five** of Mike's runs (1.83M/1.29M/1.46M/
1.75M/1.46M). Sun Wukong did 414k and died.

**WHY ‚Äî and the live content is NOT at fault.** Ice Golem 20's boss goal reads, verbatim:
> *"Avoid triggering Frigid Vengeance with burst damage ‚Äî use **Poison or HP Burn** to gradually
> reduce boss HP through thresholds."* ‚Üí solutions: `[Poison]` | `[HP Burn]` | `[Block Revive]`

That is exactly right. But goal coverage is **binary**: Ezio alone ticks `[Poison]` ‚Üí the goal reads
as SOLVED ‚Üí **Xenomorph's poison is worth ZERO more** ‚Üí the seat goes to whoever ticks another box
(Sun Wukong, `AoE Damage`, for the wave goal). The engine knows *which capability* is needed and
nothing about **how much**.

**Three faces of the same defect:**
1. **Coverage is binary** ‚Äî one poisoner solves the DoT goal; the second is valued at zero.
2. **No magnitude** ‚Äî nothing knows Xenomorph's poison is ~4√ó Sun Wukong's burst *on this content*.
   `lib/damage-mechanics.js` ¬ß1 (%maxHP DoT is DEF-independent; burst is not) is not consulted.
3. **No synergy in SELECTION** ‚Äî `detectSynergies` runs **after** `selectTeam`, so it annotates the
   team the engine already chose. It will now correctly report that the pair it failed to field is
   absent. That is a reporter, not a selector.

**Under all three:** `match-engine.js` does not read `lib/dungeon-mechanics.js` ‚Äî the file that
actually knows Ice Golem is a DoT race. The problem-model is still diagnostic-only.

## Traps (learned the hard way today)

- **The battle log is MULTI-ACCOUNT.** Scope by `displayName`/`accountId` before ANY per-account read.
- **`debug-fk-rec.mjs` prints `r.stageLabel ?? r.stageNumber`; the engine returns `stage_number_attempted`** ‚Üí it shows `stage: undefined`. The stage is fine. I reported this as an engine bug. It isn't.
- **Ad-hoc REST helpers that swallow errors will invent findings.** A `select=name` on a table with no `name` column returned a 400, my helper caught it and returned `[]`, and I nearly reported "FK 21-25 has 0 goals" as product state. It has 8 per stage. **Throw, don't swallow.**
- **ACC threshold `team ?` is BY DESIGN** ‚Äî `evaluateAccThreshold` reports `estimated_value` only as the *limiting* value on failure. `‚úì ACC need 190 team ?` means every ACC-dependent goal has a carrier above the floor. Not a bug.
- **`champion_solo_profiles.required_set` is what a champion SHOULD run, not what they ARE in.** I read Michelangelo's Toxic requirement and told Mike his team had Toxic-set poison. It doesn't. He's in Perception.
- **Don't use PowerShell here-strings (`-m @'...'@`) in the Bash tool** ‚Äî it parses as concatenation and the commit subject becomes a literal `@`. Use heredocs.
- **`timeout N cmd | tail`** masks the exit code (tail's 0 wins) ‚Äî a 580s timeout looked like success with no output.
